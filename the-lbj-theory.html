The LBJ Theory

By 1963, Lyndon B. Johnson was concerned that the scandals surrounding his cronies would ruin his hopes for the presidency. The LBJ conspiracy theory says that when rumors started circulating that JFK was going to drop him from the 1964 ticket, LBJ worked to stage an elaborate coup to kill Kennedy, enlisting loyal Texas oilmen who feared losing the oil depletion allowance and warmongers who wanted to step up involvement in Vietnam. One of their foot soldiers was Lee Harvey Oswald.

Support

  • Texas Governor ​John Connally was LBJ’s longtime friend and colleague
    • In college, Connally roomed with Dallas County district attorney Henry Wade, whose press conference on November 24, 1953 made the definitive case against Oswald​
  • The deputy ambassador to Vietnam under LBJ, once served as the attorney for the wife of J. D. Tippit, whom Oswald shot and killed soon after the assassination
  • Historically, assassinations of leaders have been carried out by their successors
  • Soon after becoming president, LBJ, a hawk, pressed the House and Senate for passage of the Gulf of Tonkin resolution, initiating a period of increased involvement in Vietnam
    • This was against what JFK would've done
  • LBJ sealed certain assassination records until 2039, ordered that Kennedy’s limousine be refurbished rather than entered into evidence, and handpicked each member of the Warren Commission, which answered ultimately to him

Opposition

  • If Connally was LBJ's longtime friend and colleague, why would he get shot along with President Kennedy?
  • There's no evidence whatsoever that LBJ had anything to do with JFK's assassination
