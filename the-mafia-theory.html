The Mafia Theory

Chicago godfather Sam Giancana helped Kennedy win the 1960 election through skulduggery and Miami mobster Santos Trafficante helped the CIA with its assassination attempts to kill Fidel Castro, but JFK launched an all-out campaign against organized crime.  Attorney General Robert Kennedy, President Kennedy's brother, first went after boss Jimmy Hoffa and then deported boss Carlos Marcello to Guatemala. The mob felt betrayed and angry at President Kennedy for going soft on Castro. The mafia conspiracy theory says that Oswald was hired by the Mafia as an assassin. Upon Oswald's arrest, the mafia sent Jack Ruby to kill and silence him.

Support

  • Between 1975 and 1976, as Congress was investigating the mafia and the CIA, Sam Giancana was gunned down in his kitchen, Jimmy Hoffa “disappeared,” and Vegas mobster Johnny Rosell, who said that Ruby was ordered to silence Oswald, was dismembered, stuffed into an oil drum, and tossed off the coast of Florida
  • Kennedy had an affair with one of Giancana's girlfriends, who claimed that she often delivered envelopes to and from President Kennedy and Giancana
  • The HSCA concluded that the big mafia bosses had "the “motive, means, and opportunity” to assassinate Kennedy
  • Hoffa once told a federal informant that he'd like to kill RFK, but that his brother, JFK, was a more desirable victim
  • Marcello once explained why it was better to target JFK than RFK
  • According to an FBI informant, in 1962, Trafficante once told him that the president “was going to be hit”
  • In 1992, a longtime lawyer of Hoffa, Marcello, and Trafficante said that the mobsters had agreed to kill the President
    • According to the lawyer, Trafficante said on his deathbed: “Carlos f***ed up. We shouldn’t have gotten rid of Giovanni [John]. We should have killed Bobby"
  • Oswald’s uncle and surrogate father was in Marcello organization, and his mother dated members of Marcello’s gang
  • When Ruby was a teenager in Chicago, he ran errands for Al Capone
    • When he became an adult, he had ties to members of the Giancana, Hoffa, Marcello, and Trafficante families
    • He visited Trafficante in his Cuban jail cell in 1959, where Castro had thrown the mobster after the revolution
  • In 1993, an Illinois cop killer, who claimed to have been a trainer of Cuban exiles for the Bay of Pigs invasion and the personal driver for a big Chicago mobster, confessed to firing from the grassy knoll

Opposition

  • The HSCA was ultimately “unable to establish any direct evidence” of the mob being involved
  • A Chicago FBI agent, who spent hours listening to wiretaps of mobsters after the assassination, said they were happy JFK was assassinated, but did not talk of a conspiracy
  • There's no proof that Ruby was anything more than a small-timer in the mafia world
  • The big mafia bosses likely wouldn't have taken such a risky move to assassinate the President, especially not relying on someone like Oswald to assassinate him
