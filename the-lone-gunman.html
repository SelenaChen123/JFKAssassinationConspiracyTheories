The Lone Gunman

​Lyndon B. Johnson became the President after JFK was assassinated. A week after LBJ became President, the President’s Commission on the Assassination of President Kennedy, also known as the Warren Commission, was created. The purpose of this commission was to investigate the assassination of JFK. An 888-page final report, called the Warren Report, was finally presented to President LBJ by this commission, concluding that JFK was assassinated by Lee Harvey Oswald and Lee Harvey Oswald alone. The Warren Commission also concluded that Jack Ruby, the one who later shot and killed Lee Harvey Oswald, also acted alone. These findings were controversial and were both challenged and supported by later investigations.

Lee Harvey Oswald


​Lee Harvey Oswald was born on October 18, 1939, in New Orleans, Louisiana. He became a US Marine in 1956 and defected to the Soviet Union for a while after becoming increasingly interested in communism. He married Marina Prusakova in 1961 and later came back to the US in 1962 to live in Dallas, Texas with Marina and their newborn.



​In early 1963, Oswald bought a handgun and a rifle. His wife Marina took a picture of him holding the rifle, which would turn out to be the firearm that shot and killed JFK. In April of 1963, Oswald allegedly tried to shoot right-wing ex-general Edwin A. Walker through the window of his home, but missed. In September 1963, Oswald went to Mexico City, where he tried to go to Cuba and the Soviet Union, but was denied passage. He then returned to the US and got a job at the Texas School Book Depository in Dallas, where he would later shoot JFK from.
On the morning of November 22, 1963, Oswald was seen holding a rifle on the sixth floor of the Texas School Book Depository. Starting at 12:30 PM, three shots were then confirmed to have been fired, one of which wounded both the president and Texas governor John Connally, another that hit the president in the head, and another that missed entirely. President Kennedy died in a hospital shortly after the attack.
A witness saw Oswald fire the fatal shot and his description of the him went out on the police radio. Oswald was later spotted leaving the scene and confronted by police. He allegedly shot and killed police officer J.D. Tippit before running away to the Texas Theater. There, he was cornered and arrested by police.
​
​Then, on November 24, two days after JFK was assassinated and before Oswald was tried in court, Oswald was shot and killed by Jack Ruby, a club owner with mob affiliations, as he was being taken to the county jail. Ruby claims to have killed Oswald out of anger over President Kennedy's assassination, but there are many theories where he was a part of a bigger scheme.
