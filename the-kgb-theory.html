The KGB Theory

After the Cuban Missile Crisis, Nikita Khrushchev allegedly wanted to punish President Kennedy. The KGB theory says that Oswald was either trained to become an assassin while living in the Soviet Union, or he was ​set up by an Oswald double and KGB officials, who fired the fatal shots at Dealey Plaza

Support

  • A Russian baron and suspected spy, who was Oswald's closest friend before he assassination, fatally shot himself in 1977, before he could testify before the HSCA
  • The uncle of Marina, Oswald'd Russian-born wife, was a ranking officer in the Soviet Ministry of Internal Affairs
  • In 1960, Oswald was given a rent-free apartment in Minsk, where an espionage training academy was located
    • Recently declassified files show that up to twenty KGB agents shadowed him and may have manipulated his behavior
  • The Russians, who had never before captured a U2 spy plane, suddenly managed to do so six and a half months after Oswald, who worked as a Marine radar operator at the air base in Japan where the U2 was based, defected to the Soviet Union
  • In September of 1963, Oswald visited the Soviet embassy in Mexico City and spoke to a KGB agent and an agent in sabotage and assassination
  • After spying on several of Oswald's hunting trips, the KGB concluded that Oswald was a poor shot

Opposition

  •  It's very unlikely that the KGB would have recruited someone as unstable as Oswald for such an important, covert operation, since the US would've launched a nuclear retaliation if the plan failed
  • Oswald was a low-level officer while at the air base in Japan, so he had little exposure to the U2
  • A 1981 exhumation of Oswald's body confirmed that it was indeed Oswald that was buried, not his Soviet-trained double
  • Marina later recanted her testimony and said her husband was innocent
    • She said that she had been threatened with deportation if she did not cooperate
  • Khrushchev wouldn't have wanted to get rid of Kennedy for Johnson, who was a more zealous anti-communist
