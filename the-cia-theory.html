The CIA Theory

By the time Kennedy became President in 1961, the CIA had spun out of control. The CIA pursued its own objectives during the Eisenhower administration. They instigated coups, incited rebellions, and tried to assassinate foreign leaders without White House supervision. When the Bay of Pigs invasion, which the CIA orchestrated, failed, President Kennedy fired the director of the CIA and the director's deputies, threatening to “smash the CIA into a thousand pieces and scatter it to the winds.” The CIA conspiracy theory says that some CIA agents, afraid of what Kennedy could do to the CIA, assassinated Kennedy either by placing CIA shooters at Dealey Plaza or by enlisting former Marine and spy wannabe Lee Harvey Oswald to assassinate him.

Support

  • The CIA director that Kennedy fired was Allen Dulles, who later served on the Warren Commission
  • One of the deputy directors that Kennedy fired was the brother of the Dallas mayor
  • One of Kennedy's alleged mistresses was married to a CIA official and was later murdered in 1964, just a year after Kennedy was assassinated
  • The CIA had the power to conceal an assassination of the President, since they did it before with leaders in Guatemala and Iran
    • Plus, without many others knowing, as Kennedy was making his way through Dallas, a CIA official was giving a poisoned fountain pen to a Cuban in Paris, who had volunteered to hand it to Fidel Castro
  • While in the Marines, Oswald was stationed at an air base in Japan that was the home of the largest CIA station in the Pacific
  • The CIA withheld so much information during the Warren Commission's investigations
    • This included information about them working together with the mafia to plot and kill Castro

Opposition

  • There's no proof that Oswald was ever involved with the CIA
    • He was just a low-level officer who was so crazy he once shot himself in the arm
  • It's hard to believe the CIA would assassinate its own President, even if they've assassinated leaders of other countries
