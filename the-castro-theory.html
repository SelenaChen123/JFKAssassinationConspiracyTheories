The Castro Theory

The US government attempted to assassinate Fidel Castro multiple times. According to the Castro conspiracy theory, after the Bay of Pigs invasion and other CIA-orchestrated attempts to kill him, Castro decided to strike back and try to kill President Kennedy. Castro recruited Oswald, a communist sympathizer, to be the assassin.

Support

  • Oswald admired Castro, often referring to him as Uncle Fidel
  • In the summer of 1963, Oswald was planning to relocate his family to Havana
  • In a September 1963 interview, Castro called Kennedy a “cretin” and threatened to retaliate against him
  • In late September of 1963, Oswald tried to obtain a Cuban visa in Mexico City
    • He presented himself as a "friend of the Cuban revolution" and some think that he even offered to be an assassin
  • A hijacker who claimed to have infiltrated Cuban intelligence in the early sixties, testified before the HSCA that he saw a file labeled “Osvaldo-Kennedy” at a Cuban intelligence facility
    • The hijacker claimed that the file contained a photo of Oswald, a KGB recommendation, and this conclusion: “Oswald is an adventurer. Our embassy in Mexico has orders to get in contact with him. Be very careful

Opposition

  • Castro would've likely been such a risky attack, since he should've known the US would retaliate if his plot was discovered
    • According to later-released NSA documents, Castro was terrified that the US would retaliate against Cuba in the first hours after the assassination
  • Oswald was denied a Cuban visa
